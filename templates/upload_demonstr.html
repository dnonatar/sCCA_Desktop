Sparse Canonical Correlation Analysis (sCCA)

The following resourses provide more information on Canonical Correlation Analysis.

CCA basics

CCA basics 2

 

Please follow the instructions below to perform your analysis.

Create two files, either a txt or csv. Each of them contains a table with rows as samples and columns and features. Note that they must have an equal number of samples, but can have different numbers of features.

Note: A feature is a measureable individual property of a phenomenon being observed such as genes or OTUs.

 

Example

  Feature 1 Feature 2 Feature 3 Feature 4
sample 1 0.12 0.33 0.24 0.35
sample 2 0.54 0.11 0.27 0.17
sample 3 0.89 0.34 0.67 0.18
